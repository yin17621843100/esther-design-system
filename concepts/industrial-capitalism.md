# Industrial Capitalism

## Summary

Industrial capitalism is the phase in which capitalist accumulation becomes tightly linked to mechanized production, factories, fossil energy, expanding commodity hinterlands, and new labor regimes. In [[capitalism-a-global-history]], Beckert presents the Industrial Revolution as capitalism's consequential acceleration, not its origin.

## Key Ideas

- Industrialization intensified production and made economic change self-sustaining.
- Industrial centers depended on global supplies of raw materials and agricultural commodities.
- The expansion of industrial capitalism intensified the need to recruit, discipline, and control labor.
- Industrial capitalism generated both capitalist civilization and powerful rebellions against older regimes of slavery, coercion, and factory discipline.
- Twentieth-century industrial capitalism required taming through political settlements, welfare institutions, and postwar growth regimes.
