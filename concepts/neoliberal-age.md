# Neoliberal Age

## Summary

The neoliberal age is the period Beckert places after 1973, when many political and economic actors moved away from postwar arrangements that had partially tamed industrial capitalism. In [[capitalism-a-global-history]], this period is introduced through Chile after the 1973 coup and broader efforts to restore market-oriented policies.

## Key Ideas

- Neoliberal policy is presented as a reaction against the postwar settlement.
- Labor power, welfare commitments, and state management of capitalism were challenged.
- Market freedom could coexist with authoritarian political force, as the Chile example suggests.
- Contemporary global production, including garment work in Cambodia, appears in the book's epilogue as part of capitalism's unresolved future.
