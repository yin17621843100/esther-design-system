# Global Capitalism

## Summary

Global capitalism refers to capitalism as a cross-regional and world-making process. In [[capitalism-a-global-history]], Beckert argues that capitalism's development depended on merchants, empires, commodity frontiers, plantation systems, industrial centers, colonial and postcolonial states, and distant labor regimes.

## Key Ideas

- Capitalist development is not contained within one nation-state.
- Trade networks connect distant producers and consumers before capitalism becomes a mass social order.
- Commodity frontiers and hinterlands supply industrial centers with raw materials and food.
- Global labor regimes include slavery, coerced labor, wage labor, household production, and postcolonial industrial work.
- The form of capitalism changes as global resistance, political reconstruction, and state action reshape it.
