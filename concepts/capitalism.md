# Capitalism

## Summary

In [[capitalism-a-global-history]], capitalism is treated as a historical process organized around capital accumulation, commodification, market exchange, transformed labor relations, and expanding global connections. Beckert emphasizes that capitalism did not appear fully formed in one place; it developed unevenly through many connected sites and conflicts.

## Key Features in This Source

- Markets become central to acquiring goods and selling labor.
- Capital accumulation becomes a structuring force in production, politics, and everyday life.
- Global commodity chains connect distant producers and consumers.
- State power, coercion, colonial expansion, and slavery are part of capitalism's historical development, not external accidents.
- Resistance and crisis repeatedly reshape capitalism's institutional form.
