  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-04-transforming-the-countryside]] - Follows capital into plantations, rural production, land, labor, and empire.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-05-intensifying-industry]] - Covers preindustrial manufacturing intensification before mechanized factories.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-06-the-perfect-storm]] - Explains the convergence that prepared industrial capitalism.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-07-the-rise-of-industrial-capitalism]] - Treats industrialization as capitalism's major acceleration.

  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-15-insurgents]] - Covers postcolonial industrial sovereignty and development.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-16-taming-industrial-capitalism]] - Describes the post-1945 taming of industrial capitalism.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-chapter-17-riding-the-tiger]] - Examines the neoliberal age from 1973 to 2008.
  - [[capitalism-a-global-history-epilogue]] - Returns to capitalism's future through Cambodian garment work and capital's escape routes.

## Concepts

- [[capitalism]] - Historical process of capital accumulation, commodification, markets, labor transformation, and global connection.
- [[global-capitalism]] - Capitalism understood as a cross-regional world-making process.
- [[industrial-capitalism]] - Capitalist phase linked to mechanized production, factories, expanded commodity hinterlands, and labor reconstruction.
- [[neoliberal-age]] - Post-1973 period of market-oriented restructuring after the postwar taming of industrial capitalism.
