# Capitalism: A Global History - Chapter 10: Rebellions: The Crisis of Old-Regime Capitalism, 1830-1870

## Summary

Chapter 10 presents rebellion as a constitutive force in capitalism's development. Revolts by enslaved people, workers, peasants, rural producers, and capital owners cracked the old regime and forced new experiments in capital, labor, and state power.

## Key Claims

- Capitalism was shaped by resistance as well as by accumulation, markets, and technology.
- Old-regime capitalism combined new capitalist dynamics with older coercive and aristocratic structures that became increasingly unstable.
- Rebellions opened political and institutional space for the later reconstruction of capitalism.
- The post-1870 order emerged through conflict and contingency rather than linear progress.

## Evidence Anchors

- Uses revolutionary struggle in slave lands as one major example of crisis.
- Treats mid-nineteenth-century upheavals as global rather than merely national episodes.
- Ends by framing the coming reconstruction as a painful and experimental process.
