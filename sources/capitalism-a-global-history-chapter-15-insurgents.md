# Capitalism: A Global History - Chapter 15: Insurgents

## Summary

Chapter 15 focuses on postcolonial insurgency and development. Beckert uses Indian industrialization and the Godrej typewriter to show how newly independent states, domestic capitalists, workers, peasants, and nationalist movements sought to redirect capitalism toward sovereignty and industrial capacity.

## Key Claims

- Decolonization fundamentally reshaped global capitalism by creating new states, development projects, and industrial ambitions.
- Postcolonial capitalists often relied on state support, procurement, protection, and planning.
- National independence linked capitalism to projects of economic sovereignty rather than simply market liberalization.
- These projects produced hope, anxiety, conflict, and openings for later critiques from neoliberal intellectuals.

## Evidence Anchors

- Opens with the typewriter as a machine of bureaucracy, business, state-building, and postcolonial self-reliance.
- Godrej's Indian typewriter becomes a symbol of industrial sovereignty after British rule.
- Ends by linking decolonization to the later emergence of critiques of postcolonial development policy.
